# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. This type of fraud usually occurs through social engineering techniques.

In a typical scenario, the attacker contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or fake advertisement, pretending to be a buyer, bank official, or customer care representative. The victim is then asked to scan a QR code or approve a “collect request” on a UPI app. Many users mistakenly believe they are receiving money, but in reality, they are authorizing a payment. Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money is instantly transferred to the attacker’s account.

Students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payment systems are the most common targets. The consequences include immediate financial loss, difficulty in recovering money, and emotional stress. In some cases, victims also lose trust in digital platforms.

To prevent such fraud, users should never share their UPI PIN, avoid scanning unknown QR codes, and carefully read payment notifications. Awareness and caution are the most effective ways to stay safe from such cybercrimes.
